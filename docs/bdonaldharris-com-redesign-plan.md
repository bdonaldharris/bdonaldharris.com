# bdonaldharris.com Complete Redesign Plan

**Document purpose:** Design and implementation reference for rebuilding `bdonaldharris.com` from scratch.  
**Primary use:** Source material for Google Stitch design prompts, Codex GitHub issue prompts, and design handoff bundles.  
**Recommended build approach:** New static React/Next.js marketing site, not a refactor of the current site.  
**Site role:** Personal authority platform for B Donald Harris.

---

## 1. Strategic Summary

`bdonaldharris.com` should become the primary personal platform for B Donald Harris: founder, builder, technologist, speaker, ecosystem architect, podcast host, and faith-rooted leader.

The site should not feel like a generic personal bio, ministry page, coaching funnel, portfolio, or consulting brochure. It should function as the narrative home for the full body of work: NotableBIT, BitVoices, HindSite, speaking, media, writing, technology leadership, community building, and lived experience.

The core repositioning is:

> B Donald Harris is building tools, platforms, conversations, and ecosystems that help Black builders move from visibility to ownership in the AI era.

The site should help visitors quickly understand:

1. Who B Donald is.
2. What he is building.
3. Why his work matters.
4. What he speaks and writes about.
5. How to collaborate, invite, book, or connect.

## 5. Core Message Pillars

The redesign should be built around these message pillars.

### Pillar 1: Builder Ownership

Visibility is important, but ownership is the deeper goal. The site should show that B Donald is focused on helping Black builders own platforms, products, narratives, and opportunities.

### Pillar 2: AI Requires Context

AI can generate output, but builders must preserve context, review decisions, and lead with understanding. This connects directly to HindSite, comprehension debt, agentic development, and AI-assisted building.

### Pillar 3: Community Is Culture

Community is not merely a set of features. It is trust, visibility, safety, mutual support, shared language, and belonging. This connects directly to BitVoices.

### Pillar 4: Faith-Rooted Leadership

Faith and ministry are part of B Donald’s leadership formation, but the redesigned site should not read primarily as a ministry site. This layer should add depth and integrity to the founder story without dominating the homepage.

### Pillar 5: Lived Experience and Clarity

B Donald’s late autism diagnosis, long engineering career, ministry background, and founder journey all inform how he sees systems, patterns, people, and technology. This should be handled with dignity and without making the site clinical or overly personal.

The visual design should feel like a premium editorial founder site with a modern technology foundation.

It should be:

- Dark, cinematic, and elegant
- Warm enough to feel human
- Technical enough to feel credible

- Designed around story, not clutter

- Premium editorial
- Founder-led
- Dark tech
- Cinematic portraiture
- Subtle Afrofuturist energy
- Warm leadership presence
- Strategic systems thinking
- Black tech excellence
- Calm authority

### Suggested Color Palette

Use a dark premium base with restrained accents.

#### Base Colors

- Deep black: `#050608`
- Midnight navy: `#08111F`
- Charcoal blue: `#111827`
- Deep slate: `#1F2937`

#### Accent Colors

- Electric blue: `#3B82F6`
- Cyan glow: `#22D3EE`
- Warm amber: `#F59E0B`
- Soft gold: `#D6A94A`

#### Text Colors

- Primary text: `#F8FAFC`
- Secondary text: `#CBD5E1`
- Muted text: `#94A3B8`
- Subtle border: `rgba(148, 163, 184, 0.20)`

### Typography Direction

Use a modern sans-serif pairing.

Recommended direction:

- Headings: bold, clean, premium sans-serif
- Body: highly readable sans-serif
- Optional accent: small uppercase labels with letter spacing

Possible font pairings:

- Inter + Space Grotesk
- Geist Sans + Space Grotesk
- Manrope + Sora
- Plus Jakarta Sans + Inter

### Imagery Direction

The site should use strong founder imagery if available. Photography should feel:

- Editorial
- Confident
- Warm
- Modern
- Not corporate stock
- Not overly staged
- Not overly ministry-coded

Use images sparingly and intentionally. A strong hero image or portrait can be enough.

### Background Motifs

Subtle motifs may include:

- Network lines
- Abstract workflow traces
- Soft gradients
- Grid patterns
- Timeline lines
- Signal/noise textures
- Light trails
- Faint code or terminal-inspired shapes

Avoid making the site look like a crypto site, generic SaaS site, or sci-fi dashboard.

## 18. Content Rules

### Current Stage Honesty

Do not imply capabilities, community outcomes, or product maturity that are not currently true.

Examples:

Use:

> I am building HindSite as workflow intelligence for builders.

Avoid:

> HindSite is already transforming how thousands of builders document their work.

Use:

> BitVoices is growing as a platform and media ecosystem for Black builders.

Avoid:

> BitVoices is the definitive global hub for Black technologists.

### Avoid Overclaiming

The site can be ambitious, but it should not sound inflated. B Donald’s actual background is strong enough.

### Keep Black Builders Explicit

For BitVoices and ecosystem work, keep “Black builders,” “Black technologists,” and “Black excellence in tech” explicit where appropriate.

### Keep Faith Integrated but Balanced

Faith is part of the story, but the homepage should not lead as a ministry site. Faith belongs in the About page, leadership story, and selected speaking topics.

### Keep Autism Language Respectful

Use identity language if referencing B Donald personally:

- “I am Autistic”
- “late autism diagnosis”
- “gave language to patterns I had carried for years”

Avoid framing autism as deficit-only, inspirational trope, or medical disclosure for its own sake.
